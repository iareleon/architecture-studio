# Video Production Guide

A practical guide for producing professional-quality video content using a smartphone
or mirrorless camera + laptop workflow. Equipment names are provided as examples — the
principles apply to any equivalent gear.

---

## Equipment Tiers

### Tier 1 — Smartphone only

**Minimum viable setup:**
- Smartphone with 4K/60fps capability (example: iPhone 15 Pro or equivalent Android flagship)
- Mini tripod or table-top stand
- Wireless clip-on microphone (example: DJI Mic 2 Mini, RØDE Wireless GO II)
- Natural window light (diffused — avoid direct sunlight)

**Best for:** talking-head content, tutorials, social media clips

### Tier 2 — Mirrorless camera + laptop

**Core gear (examples):**
- Mirrorless body: any APS-C or full-frame with clean HDMI out (example: Canon EOS R6 Mark II)
- Lens: 35mm or 50mm f/1.8 for a natural look; wide-angle (16–18mm) for desk setups
- External mic: shotgun on-camera (example: RØDE VideoMicro II) or desk condenser for voice-over
- Key light: 60W+ LED softbox or ring light positioned 45° to camera
- Fill light or reflector to soften shadows
- Capture card if using camera as webcam (example: Elgato Cam Link 4K)
- Laptop with video editing capability

**Best for:** YouTube long-form, product demos, client-facing professional content

---

## Pre-Production Checklist

- [ ] Script or bullet-point outline written (see `workflows/social-media-video.md`)
- [ ] Shot list confirmed: A-roll (presenter talking), B-roll (screen recording / closeups)
- [ ] Location checked: background clean, no distracting objects in frame
- [ ] Lighting set up and tested
- [ ] Microphone tested: no wind noise, room echo reduced (use soft furnishings)
- [ ] Camera settings: 4K / 24fps (cinematic) or 1080p / 60fps (smooth motion), manual exposure

---

## Recording Workflow

1. **Frame the shot:** eye line at or just above the camera lens; 1/3 space above head
2. **Set exposure:** expose for your face, not the background; ISO ≤ 1600 (Tier 2)
3. **Sound check:** record 30 seconds of room tone; clap once to aid sync in post
4. **Record in short takes:** each scripted segment as a separate take — easier to edit
5. **B-roll last:** record screen captures, close-ups, or supplementary clips after A-roll

---

## Post-Production

### Editing

- Import footage into editing software (examples: DaVinci Resolve free / Final Cut Pro / Premiere)
- Sync audio if using an external recorder
- Build a rough cut: A-roll first, then add B-roll over speaking gaps
- Colour grade: apply a LUT or manual grade — warm skin tones, clean shadows
- Add captions (auto-generate + review for accuracy — required for accessibility)
- Export: H.264 or H.265, 1080p minimum for YouTube; platform-specific for Shorts/Reels

### Thumbnail

- 1280×720 px minimum (YouTube); 1:1 or 9:16 for social platforms
- High contrast text (2–4 words max)
- Clear, emotive facial expression if showing a person
- Branded border or colour consistent across series

---

## Screen Recording (tutorial / demo content)

- Resolution: record at display resolution; downscale to 1080p in export
- Frame rate: 60fps for smooth cursor movement
- Tool examples: QuickTime (macOS built-in), OBS (free, cross-platform), ScreenFlow
- Clean desktop before recording: hide notifications, close unnecessary windows
- Zoom into areas of focus (3–4 second hold per zoom) — improves mobile viewing

---

## Audio Quality Rules

1. Microphone close to mouth (15–30 cm) reduces room echo
2. Treat the room: record in a closet, behind soft furnishings, or use acoustic panels
3. Remove background noise in post (DaVinci Fairlight's Noise Reduction, Adobe's Enhance Speech)
4. Target loudness: -14 LUFS integrated for YouTube; -16 LUFS for streaming / social

## Upload Checklist (YouTube example)

- [ ] Title: keyword-rich, ≤ 60 characters, enticing
- [ ] Description: first 150 characters include primary keyword and CTA; full description ≥ 250 words
- [ ] Tags: 5–10 specific + broad keyword tags
- [ ] Thumbnail uploaded (never use auto-generated)
- [ ] End screen (last 20 seconds) and cards added
- [ ] Chapter markers added in description (`0:00 Introduction`, `1:30 Topic 1`, …)
- [ ] Captions file uploaded or auto-captions reviewed
- [ ] Scheduled publish time set (see social-media-guide.md for best times)
